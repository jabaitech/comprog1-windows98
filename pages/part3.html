Design Principles of Visual Media

  • Repetition:
    Consistent use of visual elements to create unity and cohesion.
  • Center of Interest:
    The focal point of a composition that immediately draws the viewer's attention; it is the most critical aspect of visual media.
  • Balance:
    Achieving visual equality in terms of shape, form, color, and value, either through symmetry or asymmetry.
  • Harmony:
    Combining similar visual elements to create a unified and pleasing composition.
  • Contrast:
    Highlighting differences in value to create emphasis or visual tension within a design.
  • Perspective:
    Arranging objects in a two-dimensional space to simulate depth and realism, as they appear in the real world.
